Data Mesh & Data Products

Data Product:

unit of architecture that can be independently deployed with high functional cohesion, and includes all the structural elements required for its function. A data product is a logical unit that contains all components to process and store domain data for analytical or data-intensive use cases and makes them available to other teams via output ports.

  • a shift in mindset from data as a byproduct of transactional systems and processes to data purposefully designed and packaged as a “product” for an analytical consumption
  • Operational vs Analytical

Platform Services

  • Self-serve Data Platform
  • Data Product Services
    • Creation, Policies, Approvals, Versioning
  • Data Product Discovery Services
  • Data Product Auditability Services
    • Trail, Risk, Change Management

Data Mesh - Benefits & Opportunities

Culture & Mindset

  • Data is not a byproduct
  • Data is a/the Product
  • How does this change Product Discovery?

Quality

  • Shift Left - Enforcing Quality at the Source
  • AI for anomaly detection
  • AI for (automatic?) remeditation and escalation
  • Answer Keys

Security

  • Limited access by default
  • Smaller blast radius - incidents and breaches
  • Incident response

Scalability

  • Nimble, Segregated Infrastructure by Product
  • Elastic Infrastructure based on Demand
  • Reduced Data Movement
  • Optimized Costs

Governance

  • Data ownership
  • Auditability
  • Data lineage
  • Costs

Policy

  • Domains can enforce their own policies
  • Agency and flexibility at the team level

Standardization & Interoperability

  • API-driven Platform Services enables inegration & communication between systems

Data Mesh - Risks & Challenges

Culture & Mindset

  • Overemphasis on data products might overshadow other priorities
  • Resistance to change from traditional data approaches
  • Need for continuous training and mindset shifts

Quality

  • Over-reliance on AI might lead to oversight of anomalies. Risky in healthcare and financial settings
  • Increased complexity in data quality checks
  • Challenge in defining what constitutes 'quality' data

Security

  • Increased points of potential data breaches
  • Complexity in managing access across distributed data products
  • Reliance on teams to manage their own security might lead to lapses

Scalability

  • Increased complexity in managing multiple infrastructures
  • Potential for cost overruns if not optimized correctly
  • Challenges in ensuring all data products can scale as needed

Governance

  • Potential for data silos due to ownership boundaries
  • Challenges in establishing clear data lineage
  • Complexity in tracking and auditing data usage

Policy

  • Challenge in ensuring consistency across different domain policies
  • Potential for conflicts between team-level and organizational policies
  • Difficulty in monitoring and enforcing policies across domains

Standardization & Interoperability

    • Data Format Consistency
    • API Standardization
    • Schema Mismatch
    • Version Control

Execution

  • agile, piece by piece
  • baby steps with data quality - go slow, gain buy-in from stakeholders
  • start with data as a product
  • evolve platform services as you go
  • creation / policy / standards / quality >>> discovery
